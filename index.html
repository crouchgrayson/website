The literal meaning for the word bonsai is, “potted tree”. By planting a tree in a pot, it restricts the tree from growing to its full potential, causing the tree to be miniature. Shrinking the size of a tree allows for people to focus on each aspect of the tree in great detail, which renders a greater appreciation.

The culture of bonsai originated in China around 700 AD. During the Kamakura period Japan adopted a vast amount of China’s culture, including the culture of bonsai. It wasn’t until three centuries ago that the culture of bonsai began to spread to other parts of the world. Today, bonsai culture is considered an art.

There is a common misconception that in order to plant a bonsai tree you need a "special" kind of seed. The truth is that bonsai trees grow from normal size tree seeds.
